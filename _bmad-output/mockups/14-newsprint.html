Venue Guide

Adelaide Railway Station

North Terrace, Adelaide SA 5000

This guide describes what you might see, hear, and experience at each area of the station. It is designed to help visitors prepare for their journey by providing detailed sensory information about different locations within the venue. Select any section below to view the complete details.

Sound

PA announcements every 2–3 minutes. The high ceilings create echoes. Consider noise-cancelling headphones during peak hours (7:30–9:00am and 4:30–6:00pm).

Bright Light

Large skylights create bright conditions around midday. Fluorescent backup lighting throughout. Sunglasses may help.

Crowds

Busiest 7:30–9:00am and 4:30–6:00pm weekdays. Quieter mid-morning and early afternoon.

Crowds

Can get busy during peak times. Self-service ticket machines available to avoid queues.

Screens

Ticket machines have bright screens. The staff counter has softer lighting.

Loud Sounds

Train arrival announcements are loud and sudden. Trains make significant noise. Noise-cancelling headphones strongly recommended.

Sound

Automatic hand dryers can be very loud. Paper towels also available.

Smells

Cleaning products used regularly. Some air freshener scent present.

Mixed Food Smells

Multiple vendors create a mix of cooking smells. Strongest during lunch hours.

Seating

Open seating can be crowded at mealtimes. Quieter tables at the edges.
