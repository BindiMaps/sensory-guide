Adelaide Railway Station

North Terrace, Adelaide SA 5000 · Updated January 2026

This guide describes what you might see, hear, and experience at each area of the station. Select a section to view detailed sensory information that can help you prepare for your visit.

Main Concourse

Sound Light Crowds
Medium

Sound

PA announcements every 2–3 minutes. The high ceilings create echoes that can amplify sounds. Consider noise-cancelling headphones during peak hours (7:30–9:00am and 4:30–6:00pm).

Bright Light

Large skylights create bright conditions around midday. Fluorescent backup lighting is present throughout. Sunglasses may help on sunny days.

Crowds

Busiest 7:30–9:00am and 4:30–6:00pm weekdays. Quieter periods mid-morning and early afternoon. Weekend mornings are generally calmer.

Ticketing Area

Crowds Light
Low

Crowds

Can get busy during peak times. Self-service ticket machines are available to avoid queues. Staff at the counter can assist if needed.

Screens

Ticket machines have bright screens. The staff counter area has softer lighting.

Platform Access

Sound
High

Loud Sounds

Train arrival announcements are loud and can be sudden. Trains make significant noise when arriving and departing. Noise-cancelling headphones are strongly recommended in this area.

Restrooms

Sound Smells
Medium

Sound

Automatic hand dryers can be very loud. Paper towels are also available as a quieter alternative.

Smells

Cleaning products are used regularly. Some artificial air freshener scent may be present.

Food Court

Smells Crowds Sound
High

Mixed Food Smells

Multiple food vendors create a mix of cooking smells. Coffee, fried food, and baked goods are common scents. Strongest during lunch hours.

Seating

The open seating area can be crowded at mealtimes. Some quieter tables are available at the edges, away from vendors.
