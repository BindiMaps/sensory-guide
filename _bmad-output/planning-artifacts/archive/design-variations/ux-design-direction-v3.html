Sensory Guide

Adelaide Railway Station

North Terrace, Adelaide SA 5000 · Updated January 2026

About this guide

This guide describes what you might see, hear, and experience at each area of the station. Select a section to view detailed sensory information.

Main Concourse

Sound Light Crowds
Medium

Sound

PA announcements every 2–3 minutes. The high ceilings create echoes. Consider noise-cancelling headphones during peak hours (7:30–9:00am and 4:30–6:00pm).

Bright Light

Large skylights create bright conditions around midday. Fluorescent backup lighting throughout. Sunglasses may help on sunny days.

Crowds

Busiest 7:30–9:00am and 4:30–6:00pm weekdays. Quieter periods mid-morning and early afternoon. Weekend mornings are generally calmer.

Ticketing Area

Crowds Light
Low

Crowds

Can get busy during peak times. Self-service ticket machines available to avoid queues. Staff at the counter can assist if needed.

Screens

Ticket machines have bright screens. The staff counter has softer lighting.

Platform Access

Sound
High

Loud Sounds

Train arrival announcements are loud and sudden. Trains make significant noise when arriving and departing. Noise-cancelling headphones strongly recommended.

Restrooms

Sound Smells
Medium

Sound

Automatic hand dryers can be very loud. Paper towels are also available as a quieter alternative.

Smells

Cleaning products used regularly. Some artificial air freshener scent present.

Food Court

Smells Crowds Sound
High

Mixed Food Smells

Multiple food vendors create a mix of cooking smells. Coffee, fried food, and baked goods are common scents. Strongest during lunch hours.

Seating

Open seating area can be crowded at mealtimes. Some quieter tables available at the edges away from vendors.
